FIELD DAY of the Lehigh Valley Amateur Radio Club What is Field Day? Field Day is an annual event in which ham radio operators from MANY countries participate. The major goal of Field Day is to practice operating equipment under emergency conditions.  That means, using non-commercial power (generators, solar and/or batteries), and portable antennas which are not permanently mounted. Amateurs attempt to make as many contacts as possible in a 20 hour period.   This emergency preparedness drill/contest is sponsored by the American Radio Relay League (ARRL) which is the national organization for amateur radio operators in the United States. Stations accumulate points for each “contact” they make and are sent to the ARRL for comparison with other stations around the US of similar size and operation.
