About the Lehigh Valley Amateur Radio Club The Lehigh Valley Amateur Radio Club was started in 1956 making  it one of the oldest ham radio clubs in the area.  The W3OI   (LVARC) club has regular club meetings and monthly activities.  They have a web site and a monthly newsletter (The VOX) which can be read directly from the web site.   Hams and non-hams are invited to visit the site and spend as much time as they need to view the newsletters, meeting dates and activities as well as links to other ham radio related sites! The web address of the LVARC web page is            http://www.w3oi.org The LVARC has close ties to and works closely with the Lehigh County Emergency Management Agency. Meetings of the  LVARC The LVARC meets the FIRST TUESDAY of EVERY MONTH usually at the Red Cross Building in the Industrial Park across from the L.V. Airport at 7:30 pm.  Occasionally, the meetings are held in other places.   The LVARC also has a different kind of meeting too.  That is, members meet “on the air” every Monday Night at 7:30 for the Lehigh County RACES/ARES Net.  The public can listen to these nets on 146.940 MhZ. Activities of the LVARC Ham radio activities include  “Fox” or Transmitter Hunts, personal contesting and “DXing,”  and the annual naton-wide event, Field Day.  This is a contest which involves contacting as many other amateur stations as possible running under emergency conditions.   That means no commercial power, just generators or battery power and erecting temporary antennas.  The contest is held the last weekend in June and runs for 24 hours. Members of the LVARC also serve the public by providing communications support for many community events. As well as being a club related to ham radio, the LVARC is also a social club.  Members meet for picnics, dinners and sporting events.
